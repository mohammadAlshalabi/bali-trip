The Spots

Where to go

Farsha Mountain Lounge

Cafe
Hadaba · clifftop

Multi-level Bedouin-style cafe carved into the cliff, lanterns, carpets, cushions — the most iconic sunset spot in all of Sharm. Open 11 AM – 1 AM. Go for mint tea and stay for the view.

Find on Maps

Al Sahaba Mosque

Culture
Sharm El Maya · Old Market area

One of Egypt's most beautiful modern mosques — a blend of Fatimid and Ottoman design. Free entry, modest dress required (they provide covers).

Find on Maps

Old Market (Sharm El Maya)

Shopping
The real Egyptian bazaar

Spices, lanterns, silver, perfumes, papyrus, leather. Bargain hard — start at 30% of first price. Best in the late afternoon. Walking distance from Al Sahaba Mosque.

Find on Maps

Fares Seafood

Dinner
Inside the Old Market

A Sharm legend for fresh grilled fish and generous portions. No alcohol, no fuss. Come hungry — best in the evening when it's buzzing.

Find on Maps

Soho Square

Evening
Nabq Bay area

Open-air entertainment square with a nightly dancing fountain show (~9 PM, free), ice cream, shisha cafes, bowling, kids' rides. Totally family-friendly.

Find on Maps

Rangoli

Dinner
Movenpick Resort · Naama Bay

Widely regarded as one of the best restaurants in Sharm — top-tier Indian cuisine with panoramic sunset views over the bay. Elegant setting. Reservations recommended.

Find on Maps

Pomodoro

Italian
Naama Bay · main strip

Charming Italian with a cozy terrace on the Naama Bay street. Homemade pizzas, fresh pasta, excellent coffee. Warm service, family-friendly, reasonable prices.

Find on Maps

Naama Bay Promenade

Walk
The main tourist strip

Pedestrian promenade along the beach — cafes, shops, shisha lounges, ice cream. Lively but family-friendly, especially before 9 PM. Great for an evening stroll.

Good to know

i

Taxis

100–200 EGP per ride between zones. Agree the price before getting in — meters aren't the norm.

ii

Tipping

Expected everywhere — 10–20 EGP for small services, 50–100 EGP for drivers and guides.

iii

Dress

Modest clothes in Old Market and Al Sahaba Mosque. Swim and beachwear are fine at hotels and private beaches.

iv

Visa

Free Sinai-only entry stamp on arrival at Sharm airport for Jordanians (valid 15 days). No need for a paid visa if you stay in the Sinai peninsula.

v

Book tours in town

Hotel tour desks are expensive. Walk into any local tour office in Naama Bay for the same trips at half the price.

vi

Stay hydrated

Late May sun is intense. Drink bottled water only, and carry sunscreen everywhere.
